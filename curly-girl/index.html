What is the Curly Girl Method?

Created by hairstylist Lorraine Massey and published in her 2001 book, the Curly Girl Method (CGM) is a hair care routine designed to enhance your natural curl pattern. The core idea: stop using ingredients that damage curls and start working with your natural texture.

Avoid: Sulphates

Harsh cleansers that strip your hair's natural oils. Look for names ending in "-sulfate" or "-sulphate".

  • Sodium Lauryl Sulfate (SLS)
  • Sodium Laureth Sulfate (SLES)
  • Ammonium Lauryl Sulfate

Avoid: Non-Soluble Silicones

Coat hair and block moisture. Need sulphates to remove, creating a damaging cycle.

  • Dimethicone
  • Cyclomethicone
  • Amodimethicone

Avoid: Drying Alcohols

Evaporate quickly and wick moisture from hair, leaving it dry and brittle.

  • Alcohol Denat
  • SD Alcohol
  • Isopropyl Alcohol

Safe: Fatty Alcohols

Not all alcohols are bad. Fatty alcohols are moisturising and conditioning.

  • Cetyl Alcohol
  • Cetearyl Alcohol
  • Stearyl Alcohol

Safe: Water-Soluble Silicones

Silicones with PEG or PPG prefixes wash out with gentle cleansers.

  • PEG-8 Dimethicone
  • Dimethicone Copolyol

Sneaky Exception

Behentrimonium Methosulfate sounds like a sulphate but is actually a gentle conditioning agent. CGM approved.
