What is the Curly Girl Method?

Created by Lorraine Massey in 2001, CGM is a hair care approach built on a simple idea: stop using ingredients that damage curls and start working with your natural texture.

The method avoids harsh sulphates, non-soluble silicones, and drying alcohols. It replaces them with gentle cleansers, rich conditioners, and styling products that enhance your natural curl pattern.

Avoid: Sulphates

Harsh cleansers that strip your hair's natural oils. Look for names ending in "-sulfate" or "-sulphate".

Avoid: Non-Soluble Silicones

Coat hair and block moisture. Need sulphates to remove, creating a damaging cycle.

Safe: Fatty Alcohols

Not all alcohols are bad. Cetyl, cetearyl, and stearyl alcohols are moisturising and conditioning.

Sneaky Exception

Behentrimonium Methosulfate sounds like a sulphate but is actually a gentle conditioning agent. CGM approved.
